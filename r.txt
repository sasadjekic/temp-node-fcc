 @Frankie Ramos  Went to college a while ago for Computer Science but experience is much more important than school IMHO. Freelanced for years with satisfied customers helped me get in the door along with some strong personal projects and short term contracts. Once you can put meaningful experience on your resume your all set.

Not sure if you're asking but I'll give some advice for anyone looking to break in the field.

Start small. Unless you are an expert at algorithms or have a strong resume most larger companies will ignore you. 

Take those coding challenges seriously.  If a company is giving you an app or coding challenge don't just do the minimum.  I always included extra things in mine just to stand out. If you want to be a pro act like one.

Code like a real engineer.  That means not only should you're code work it should be easy to read,  efficient,  documented and tested. Show you know what you're doing and be prepared to explain your decisions. 

You're people skills are important too!! You're going to go through a ton of interviews before you find the right fit. Practice your interviewing skills as much as you can during this time so you can be  at the top of your game.

Finally, it's a numbers game. I went through a ton of interviews before I found my first gig. Be prepared for a lot of rejection when you're starting out. Once you get that experience it's much much easier.

Anyway,  hope that helps someone.

One more thing just came to mind. Learn Git like your life depends on it because it does. I mean really learn it. Working on a repo by yourself is nothing like working with a team of engineers.
